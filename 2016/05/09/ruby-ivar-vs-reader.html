Ruby: ivar vs readers

Spoiler alert: Readers win.

  1. You don't rely on the instance state.
  2. You've better protection againt typos. @ivar might not exist and nothing will fail ivar will complain.
  3. They're easier to mock in specs in case you need to.
  4. They properly wrap the state so that refactors are easier.
  5. They can be aliased.
  6. They can be alias_method_chained.
  7. No kittens will die.
  8. You can properly set their accessibility.

When should I use ivars?

  1. When setting things on the constructor.
  2. When memoizing stuff (and be careful).

When NOT to use ivars?

  1. To pass data from a method to another.
  2. To store stuff inside the same method.

What??? But I don't do it like that!!!

Up to you. There are not rules written on stone or anyting but they work, and they do it well. It is not a matter of taste.

From my own little experience the more you isolate yourself from the class state the easier it will be to change the class when you need to. In codebases which change a lot, this introduces a very good advantage in your favor.
